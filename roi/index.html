How this is calculated (method & assumptions)
  • Total annual fuel = fleet size × average annual fuel per vehicle.
  • Litres saved = total annual fuel × reduction %.
  • Fuel cost saved = litres saved × fuel price per litre.
  • CO₂ avoided = litres saved × emission factor ÷ 1000 → tonnes. Factors: diesel 2.68 kg/L, petrol 2.31 kg/L (standard tank-to-wheel combustion factors).
  • EU ETS cost avoided = CO₂ avoided (t) × EU ETS price (€/t) — added to the total only for marine / shipping fleets, who surrender EU ETS allowances directly.
  • Total annual saving = fuel cost saved (+ EU ETS cost avoided for marine fleets only).

Only operators directly liable under EU ETS — today, maritime shipping — can bank the avoided carbon cost as cash. For road, rail and power-generation fleets the CO₂ you avoid is a genuine Scope 1 reduction — a real reporting and ESG asset — but not a second stream of euros, so this tool counts it in the total only when you select a marine fleet. The avoided tonnes are always shown for your Scope 1 report.

Indicative estimate for planning purposes only. Actual savings depend on duty cycle, vehicle mix, baseline fuel consumption, verified reduction achieved, fuel and carbon prices, and applicable regulation. Figures are not a guarantee.
